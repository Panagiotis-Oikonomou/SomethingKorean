그래도 conjunction

Description:

그래도 is used to mean "but still", "even if" or "regardless". It's often used to indicate that something is true or continues to be the case despite a previous statement or expectation.

Youtube link
You can use it just as 그래도 or the short version below.

Structure:
Present tense: Present stem + 도
이다: (1)이어도/여도 (2)이라도/라도
The (2) is used more in spoken Korean.

Examples:
비가 와 그래도 기분이 좋아요.
It's raining but still my mood is good.

배가 안 불러도 그만 먹어요.
Even if you are not full, stop eating.

천재라도 이 문제는 못 풀 거예요.
Even if you are a genius you won't solve this problem.
