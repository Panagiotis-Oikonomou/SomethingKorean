그래서/그러니까 conjunctions

A. Description:

그래서/그러니까 means "so" or "therefore". It is used to show a cause-and-effect relationship between two sentences or ideas. It's similar to how we use "so" in English to explain a result.

그래서 and 그러니까 differences
(1) General Reason (2) In the context where the listener is already aware of wha's stated as a reason
(3) Reason for the emotion verbs: "고맙다, 감사하다, 미안하다, 반갑다" (4) When the next sentence is in imperstive mood (command, suggestion etc)
You can use it just as 그래서/그러니까 or the short version below. If it is past or present tense use the same present tense for 그러니까.

Structure:
Present tense: Base stem + 서
Present tense: Base stem + (으)니까

Examples:
많이 먹어서 배가 아파요.
I ate a lot, so my stomach hurts.

(3)만나서 반가워요.
Nice to meet you.

(4)밖에 와 그러니까 우산을 가져가요.
It is raining outside so take an umbrella.

(4)심심하니까 게임 할래요?
I am bored so do you want to play a game?.
You can also use 그러니까 to emphasize the reason.

A: 왜 울어요?
     Why are you crying?
B: 슬프니까 울어요.
      I cry because I am sad.

A: 왜 날 따라와요?
     Why are you following me?
B: 사랑하니까요.
      Because I love you.
