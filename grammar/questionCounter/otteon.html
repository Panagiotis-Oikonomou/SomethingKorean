무슨 & 어느 & 어떤

Description:

"무슨" functions as a determiner and is used to ask about the kind or type of something when the specific options are not clear or known. It's often translated as "what kind of" or "what".

Youtube Link
Examples:
무슨 책 읽고 있어요?
What book are you reading?

무슨 노래 듣고 들어요?
What song are you listening to?

무슨 말이예요?
무슨 소리예요?
What do you mean? / What does that mean?

Description:

"어느" functions as a determiner similar to "which" or "which one". It is used to ask about a specific item or option from a limited, known set of choices. It implies a selection from a defined range, rather than a general or unspecified choice.

1. When you are given choices
어느 거 먹을래?
Which one do you want to eat?

어느 나라 사람이예요?
어느 나라에서 왔어요?
Where are you from?

2. Unspecified/Undentified
어느 여자가 울고 있어요.
A woman is crying.

Description:

"어떤" translates to "what kind of" or "some" depending on the context. It's used to ask about the type or nature of something, or to refer to an unspecified thing.

1. What kind of (trait, personality)
어떤 책 좋아해요?
What kind of book do you like?

제프씨는 어떤 사람이예요?
What kind of person is Jeff?

2. Which (= 어느)
어떤 거/어느 거?
Which one?

어떤 과일 먹을래요?
어느 과일 먹을래요?
Which fruit shall we eat?
"어떤" is used more often in spoken word


3. Unspecified/Undentified
어떤 여자가 울고 있어요.
어느 여자가 울고 있어요.
A woman is crying.
"어떤" is used more often in spoken word
