Counter Words

Description:

Korean counters, called "수분류사", are used with numbers to specify what is being counted. They are essential for accurate counting in Korean and differ based on the type of object or concept.

When you are using the numbers one, two, three and four you need to change them.
하나  ⇒  한, 둘  ⇒  두
셋  ⇒  세, 넷  ⇒  네
Youtube link
Structure:

Noun + Native Number + Counter Word

Examples:
1. 개 — General counter for things, animals, or objects
저는 강아지 한 개 있어요.
I have one dog.
2. 명 — Counter for people
우리 가족 네 명이에요.
There are four members in my family.
3. 권 — Counter for books, volumes
소설 세 권 샀어요.
I bought 3 novels.
4. 마리 — Counter for animals
고양이 두 마리 있어요.
There are two cats.
5. 대 — Counter for vehicles, machines, and large items
차 한 대 있어요.
I have one car.
6. 병 — Counter for bottles
물 두 병 주세요.
Please give me two bottles of water.
7. 장 — Counter for sheets of paper, tickets, or drawings
종이 세 장 있어요.
There are three sheets of paper.
8. 살 — Counter for age or years of a pesron's life
저는 25살이에요.
I am 25 years old.
9. 송이 — Counter for flowers, grapes, etc
포도 세 송이 있어요.
There are three bunches of grapes.
10. 층 — Counter for floors of a building
우리 회사는 5층에 있어요.
Our company is on the 5th floor.
11. 시간 — Counter for hours
수업 두 시간이 있어요.
The class is two hours long.
12. 그루 — Counter for trees
나무 세 그루 있어요.
There are three trees.
13. 조각 — Counter for pieces, slices, or chunks (usually food)
피자 한 조각 주세요.
Please give me one slice of pizza.

케이크 두 조각 먹었어요.
I ate two pieces of cake.
14. 켤래 — Counter for pairs (shoes, gloves, etc).
장갑 한 켤레 주세요.
Please give me one pair of gloves.
15. 벌 — Counter for clothes
셔츠 한 벌 샀어요.
I bought one shirt.
16. 자루 — Counter for long objects (pens, pencils, etc).
펜 두 자루 주세요.
Please give me two pens.
17. 마디 — Counter for parts of the body (such as joints)
손가락 한 마디 다쳤어요.
I hurt one finger joint.
