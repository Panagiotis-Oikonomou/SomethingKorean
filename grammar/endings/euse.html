(으)세요 Ending

Description:

(으)세요 is a Korean ending that expresses a polite or honorific command or request. It's used when speaking to someone older than you or someone you need to show respect to. Essentially, it's the polite way of saying "please do" or "you should". You never use it for yourself.

Structure:
Present Tense: base stem-(으)세요
Past Tense: past stem-(으)셨어요
Future Tense: base stem-(으)실 거예요

Separate Honorific Form
먹다 드세요
아프다 편찮으세요
자다 주무세요
말하다 말씀하세요
있다 계세요
Examples:
A: 선생님이 왜 안 오세요?
     Why isn't the teacher coming?
B: 바쁘세요.
     She is busy.

A: 한국인이세요?
     Are you Korean?
B: 아뇨 중국사람이에요.
     No. I am Chinese.

어머니께서 곧 오실 거예요.
My mom will come home soon.

사장님께서 크게 웃으셨어요.
My boss laughed out loud.
있다

1. stay, 2. there is (a preson)  ⇒  계세요

3. to have  ⇒  있으세요

Examples:
(2)할머니께서 집에 계세요.
My grandmother is at home.

(3)현금 있으세요?
Do you have money?

주다

주시다: show respect to the giver

드리다: show respect to the receiver

Examples:
커피 한 주세요.
One cup of coffee.

선생님께 선물을 드려요.
I gave a gift to my teacher.
