Indirect Quotation Ending

Description:

This grammar point is used in informal speech to report or relay what someone else has said, heard, or is thinking. It's essentially a way to express indirect speech, often used in conversations to explain or share what another person thinks, believes, or has said. It's the abriveted form of the Indirect Quotation.

Structure:
Present tense:
Verb stem-ㄴ/는다고 했다  ⇒  ㄴ/는대
Adjective stem + 다고 했다  ⇒  대
Noun + (이)라고 했다  ⇒  (이)래

Past tense:
Verb/Adjective/이다 + 았/었다고 했다  ⇒  대

Fututre tense:
Verb/Adjective/이다-ㄹ/을 거라고 했다  ⇒  래

Examples:
제주도가 예쁘대요.
I hear that Jeju island is pretty.

한국에서 망고가 비싸대요.
I hear that in Korea mangos are expensive.

은지가 스무 살이래요.
Eunji said she is 20 years old.

엄마는 제가 노래를 못 한대요.
Mom said I am a terrible singer.

아빠가 한 시간 후에 잘 거래요.
Dad said he will go to sleep an hour later.
