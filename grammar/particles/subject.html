은/는 Topic Markers
Description:

These particles are used to introduce a topic, or when you want to contrast or emphasize something.

이/가 Subject Markers

Description:

These particles mark the subject of the sentence or focus on a specific element. It's typically used when you're providing new information or identifying something/someone for the first time. 이/가 focuses more on the subject itself without the contrastive or introductory nuance that 은/는 brings.

Structure:
Noun-은/는
Noun-이/가

Examples:
저는 한국어를 배웠어요.
I am learning Korean.
Focus is on "learning Korean", what did you learn? Korean (nothing else).


A: 누가 한국어를 배웠어요?
     Who learned Korean?
B: 제가 했어요.
      I did.
Focus is on "I". Imagine asking that to a room full of people. In order to emphasze that you (and not the other people) have learned Korean.


저는 한국어를 공부해요.
I study Korean.
(as for me) is marking "I" as the topic, so the sentence is more about talking about what I do.


제가 한국어를 공부해요.
I study Korean.
제가 marks "I" as the subject of the sentence, putting emphasis on who is performing the action.
