-한테/에게, -께 and -께서 Particles

Description:

In Korean, -한테 and -에게 are particles that mean "to" or "from" someone, depending on the context. They are used to indicate the recipient or target of an action. -께 is the honorific version of -한테/에게, meaning "to" someone (honorific). It's used to show respect when the recipient of an action is someone of higher status (like elders, teachers, or customers). -께서 is the honorific version of the subject marker 이/가. It's used when the subject of the sentence is someone you should show respect to the same with -께.

-한테: more casual, often used in speech.
-에게: more formal or written.
이/가  →  -께서
은/는  →  -께서는

Examples:
제프에게 선물을 줬어요.
I gave a present to Jeff.

누구한테 말했어요?
To whom did you tell?

누구에게 돈을 빌렸어요?
From whom did you borrow money?

누구한테 들었어요?
From who did you hear it?

선생님께 들렸어요.
I gave it to the teacher.

어머니께 전화를 받았어요.
I got a call from my mother.

의사 선생님께서 저한테 약을 주셨어요.
The doctor gave me medicine.

할아버지께서 편찮으세요.
Grandfather is sick.

학교에 말했어요.
I told it to school.

회사에 이메일을 보냈어요.
I sent an email to the company.
