Assumption

Description:

The first grammar pattern is used to express a guess or inference based on evidence or what you've seen/heard. The second grammar pattern is a general guess based on observation or assumption. The third grammar pattern is a combined structure that links evidence with an inference. This pattern makes your guess sound reasonable and based on actual observation, rather than just assuming something randomly.

(1)Adjective/이다 stem-ㄴ/은 모양이다

(2)Verb stem + 나 보다
(2)Adjective/이다 stem-ㄴ/은가 보다

(3)(basis, why you made that assumption)
Verb stem-는 걸 보니(까) (assumption)Verb stem-는 모양이다
(3)(basis, why you made that assumption)
Adjective/이다 stem-ㄴ/은 걸 보니(까) (assumption)Adjective/이다 stem-ㄴ/은 모양이다

Examples:
(1)밖에 비가 오는 모양이에요.
It looks like it's raining outside.

(1)그 사람은 운동을 자주 하는 모양이에요.
It seems like he/she exercises often.

(2)민수 씨는 어제 많이 마시나 봐요.
It seems Minsu drank a lot yesterday.

(2)민수 씨는 피곤한가 봐요.
I guess Minsu is tired.

(3)그 사람이 계속 웃는 걸 보니까 기분이 좋은 모양이에요.
Seeing that he's smiling continuously, it seems like he's in a good mood.

(3)학생들이 조용히 공부하는 걸 보니 시험이 가까운 모양이에요.
Seeing that students are studying quietly, it seems like exams are near.
