Showing regret

Description:

The first grammar point is used to express regret or wish about something that should have been done in the past, but wasn't. It translates roughly to "I should have [verb]" or "I wish I had [verb]". It indicates that the speaker feels that it would have been better to take a particular action, but now it's too late.

The second grammar point is used to show regret about a past action or situation.

The third grammar point is used to express that something should have been done in the past, but wasn't. It conveys a sense of regret or necessity, implying that the speaker believes a certain action was required or should have been taken at a particular point in time, but wasn't.

Structure:
(1) Verb stem-ㄹ/을 걸 그랬다
(2) 괜히 + Verb
(3) Verb-았/었어야 했다

Examples:
괜히 기다렸어요.
I regret waiting.

그 사람에게 연락할 걸 그랬어요.
I should have contacted that person.

좀 더 공부할 걸 그랬어요.
I regret studying more.

그때 말할 걸 그랬어요.
I should have said it at that time.

그 사람을 만났어야 했어요.
I should have met that person.

더 많이 먹었어야 했어요.
I should have eaten more.
