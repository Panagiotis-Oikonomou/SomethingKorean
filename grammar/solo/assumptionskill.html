Assumption, Skill or Ability

Description:

The first grammar point means "to know how to [do something]" or "to be able to [do something]". It expresses ability or knowledge about how to do something. The second grammar point has two meanings:

a. I knew that would happen (I knew it, stress the 알았다, it works only with future tense)

b. I thought that would happen (but it turns out not)

Structure:
(1)Verb stem-ㄹ/을 줄 알다

(2)Present Tense
Verb/Adjective/이다-는, ㄴ/은, 인 줄 알았다/모르다

(2)Past Tense
Verb/Adjective/이다-ㄴ/은, 인 줄 알았다/모르다

(2)Future Tense
Verb/Adjective/이다-ㄹ/을 줄 알았다/모르다


Examples:
먹을 줄 알았어요.
I knew you would eat it.

너무 어려 보여서 학생인 줄 알았어요.
You look so young so I thought you were a student.

한글을 배울 때는 한국어가 쉬운 줄 알았어요.
When I was learning 한글, I thought Korean is easy but I entered intermidiate grammar and it's hard.

너무 맛있어서 전무 요리사가 만든 줄 알았어요.
It's so delicious so I thought that a profesional chef made it.

오늘 비가 올 줄 몰랐어요.
I didn't know it would rain today.

오늘 비가 오는 줄 몰랐어요.
I didn't know it is raining now.

배 고픈 줄 몰랐어요.
I didn't know you were hungry.
