Feelings in 3rd person

Description:

This grammar point is used to describe someone else's emotions, feelings, or states that you observe. The adjective should be some kind of feeling and it's most used with (he, she, they, you subject).

엄마가 슬퍼요 I can see through my mom's feelings.

1. Implies you are 100% certain about. An author knows the feelings of his characters.

2. Future tense, you predict.

3. When asking how a person feels.

엄마가 슬퍼해요: You are the observer, with your eyes, you see that mom is sad, based on her actions, face, expression etc.

1. Asking how third person (he, she, they) feel

2. You have notice a tendacy when you are doing something.

Structure:
Adjective-아/어하다

Examples:
그 아이가 무서워해요.
That child seems scared.

아이들이 베 고파해요.
The children are happy.

고양아가 추워해서 이불을 덮어줬어요.
My cat felt cold so I covered her with a blanket.

저한테 고마워하는 사람들이 많아요.
There are many people who are thankful to me.

너 이 인형 갖고 싶어했잖아요.
You and I know that you wanted to have this doll.
