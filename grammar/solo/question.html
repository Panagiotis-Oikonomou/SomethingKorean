How to Ask Questions

1. Sentence?

It' a direct question and you raise the pitch of your voice in the end.


2. -습니까?

Description:

This word is used at the end of a verb or adjective to form a polite question. It's typically used in formal or respectful situations.


Examples:
그 사람은 압니까?
Do you know this person?

아침을 먹습니까?
Do you eat breakfast?

3. -니 (반말)     -나요     -가요 (존댓말)

Description:

i. -니 is informal and is used with people you're familiar with, such as close friends or younger people.


ii. -나요 is polite and is used when you want to ask someone in a respectful but not overly formal way. You might use it with people who are a little older or in more neutral settings.


iii. -가요 is very polite and is often used with people you respect, such as elders, superiors, or in formal situations. It's common in everyday conversations when you're speaking politely but not excessively formal.


4. -ㄹ/을까

Description:

-ㄹ/을까 is a verb ending that is used to express a suggestion or a question about the speaker's own action or a joint action with the listener. It can be translated into English as "shall we?", "I wonder if I should...", or "what if I...?".


Examples:
우리 영화 볼까요?
Shall we watch a movie?

지금 갈까?
Shall I go now?

5. -지?

Description:

With -지 you express uncertainty or seek confirmation


Examples:
그 사람은 한국 사람이었지?
That person was Korean, right?

이거 맞지?
This is correct, right?

6. -래?

Description:

With -래 you suggest or offer someone to do something similar to "would you like to".


Examples:
우리 영화 보러 갈래?
Do you want to go watch a movie?

같이 점심 먹으러 갈래?
Do you want to go eat lunch together?
