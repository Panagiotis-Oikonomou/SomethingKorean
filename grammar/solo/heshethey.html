He/She/They

Description:

In Korean "he" is often "그", "she" is "그녀" and "they" is "그들". However, these are more commonly used in writing or formal settings, and less so in everyday speech. In casual conversation, Koreans often use the person's name or descriptive phrase like "that person" (그 사람) instead of pronouns. For more polite situations, they might use it like this (그 분). You can also use "걔" for "that person" out of your sight, "얘" for "this person" and "쟤" for "that person" within your sight.

With "그" it means "that person" out of your sight, but with "저" it means "that person" within your sight and with "이" it means "this person". You add "들" in the end to make it into plural.

Examples:
A: 지수는 남자친구가 있어요. 걔 알아?
Jisoo has a boyfriend. Do you know him?
B: 응. 걔랑 같은 고등학교 다녔어요.
Yes. I went to the same highschool with him.

이 분은 우리 엄마에요.
She is my mother.

저 분 누구세요?
That person who is it?

이 분들은 모두 친절해요.
These people are my senior.

A: 지수가 어떻게 A를 받았어요?
     How did Jisoo get an A?
B: 걔 공부(되게, 진짜, 매우) 열심히 했어요.
     She studied very hard.

스미스씨는 제 이웃이에요.
Mr.Smith is my neighbor.

그 분은 되게 웃기세요.
그 아저씨는 되게 웃기세요.
스미스씨는 되게 웃기세요.
He is very funny.
