(1) What is a Passive Verb (피동사)?

Active: Mosquitoes bite people. ⇒ 모기는 사람을 물어요.

Passive: People are bitten by mosquitoes. ⇒ 사람은 모기에 물려요.

Structure:
Subject + 은/는/이/가 Actor + 에/에게/한테/에 의해Passive Verb

Subject: receiver of action
에: non living things, Organizations, big group of people, Country
에게/한테: only for a person or animals
에 의해: formal situations

(2) How to change Active Verbs into Passive Verbs

1. Adding the suffix "이/ 리/히/ 기"

Verb-이다: stem ends in vowel, ㅎ 받침, ㄲ 받침, ㅍ 받침
Verb-리다: stem ends in ㄹ 받침
Verb-히다: stem ends in ㄱ 받침, ㅂ 받침
Verb-기다: stem ends in ㄴ 받침, ㅁ 받침, ㅅ 받침, ㅊ 받침
ㅈ/ㄷ: 히다/기다 it depends on the verb

Verbs where 이/리/히/기 CANNOT be attached

A. intransitive Verbs(일어나다, 자다, 울다, 웃다 etc)

Transitive verbs have both acting and receiving end the subject transfers the action to the object.

Active
바람이 창문을 열었어요.
The wind opened the window.
Wind did the action of opening and the affected window received the action.


친구가 매일 저를 꼬집어요.
My friend pinch me every day.

Passive
창문이 바람에 여렸어요.
The window opened by the wind.

저는 친구한테 매일 꼬집혀요.
I get pinched by my friend every day
The subject does the action but he can't transfer it to an object

B. 하다 ending verbs (취소하다, 수정하다 etc)

C. Exceptions aming transitive verb (만들다, 보리다, 켜다, 깨다, 치다, 고치다 etc)
